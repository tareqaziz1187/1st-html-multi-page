Why cat become angry

Cats can become angry or display aggressive behavior for a variety of reasons. It's important to understand that a cat's aggression is usually a response to a perceived threat or stress, and it's a natural instinct for them. Here are some common reasons why a cat might become angry or aggressive:

Fear or Anxiety: Cats may become aggressive when they feel threatened or scared. This can happen when they encounter unfamiliar people or animals, or when they are in an unfamiliar or uncomfortable environment.

Pain or Discomfort: A cat in pain or discomfort may react aggressively if someone touches or approaches them, especially in the area that is causing them pain.

Territorial Aggression: Cats are territorial animals, and they may become aggressive when other cats or animals intrude on their territory.

Play Aggression: Sometimes, what appears to be anger or aggression is actually play behavior. Cats can get overstimulated during play and may use their claws or teeth more aggressively, which can be mistaken for anger.

Redirected Aggression: If a cat is agitated or frustrated by something it can't reach or interact with directly, it may take out its aggression on the nearest person or animal.
